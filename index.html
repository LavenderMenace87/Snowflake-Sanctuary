The Foundation

Five Values. One Standard.

Every structure, system, and person at Snowflake Sanctuary is evaluated against the same five principles. This is not a list of rules — it is a statement of what this place is.

🔥 Creativity

Not only art. The way you solve problems, build systems, imagine new forms.

🌱 Growth

Personal, ecological, communal. Things that stop growing, die.

🌍 Land Stewardship

You tend this land. You return more than you take.

✦ Beauty

A standard of craft. The evidence that something was made with intention.

⚖️ Responsibility

You show up for your commitments. To the land. To the people. To the agreements.

Architecture

Buried & Floating

The design identity of Snowflake Sanctuary is built on contrast: structures that go deep into the earth and structures that rise above it. Ancient forms and futuristic ones. The tension between them is the story.

↓ Buried

Earth-sheltered, thermally stable, ancient form

  • Earth dome / hobbit hole · 800 SF · 32 ft diameter
  • Hobbit door entry · loft mezzanine overlooks interior
  • Mushroom tunnel below grade
  • Fungal composting hub adjacent
  • Root cellar / cool storage
  • 55–65°F year-round without mechanical heating
  • Adobe, earthbag, rammed earth, cob
  • Aquaponics tanks in lower dome level

↑ Floating

Elevated, lightweight, futuristic form

  • Floating platforms over crescent pools
  • Treehouse along the wash corridor
  • 2 elevated sleeping pods · off-ground posts
  • Suspended bridge between elevated points
  • Chinampa-style floating garden rafts
  • Geoship geodesic ceramic domes (residents)
  • Seasonal meditation platform on main pond
  • Central station · elevated deck for green screen
