The Covenant

Snowflake Sanctuary Community Constitution · Adopted 2026
WHEREAS, we, the Founders and Members of Snowflake Sanctuary, recognize that land is held in stewardship rather than dominion, and that a community flourishes through shared purpose, honest labor, beauty, responsibility, and regeneration;

WHEREAS, Snowflake Sanctuary exists as a regenerative land lab, intentional community, and place of learning, creation, and mutual aid rooted in the high desert of Snowflake, Arizona;

NOW, THEREFORE, we do hereby establish this Constitution as the governing charter of Snowflake Sanctuary.
Articles
I. Identity & Purpose II. Founding Principles III. The Land IV. Membership V. Governance VI. The Founders VII. Land Use VIII. Housing IX. Work & Contribution X. Commons & Revenue XI. Additional Articles Signatures
Article I
Identity and Purpose
1.1Snowflake Sanctuary is a private intentional community organized for regenerative living, ecological stewardship, creative work, education, practical self-reliance, and mutually beneficial community life.
1.2The purpose of the Community is to build and sustain a place where people may live, work, create, and learn in a manner consistent with the long-term flourishing of the land and the people responsible for it.
1.3The Community shall prioritize closed-loop systems, soil building, water harvesting, humane food production, beauty in design, and durable structures and practices that strengthen the land rather than deplete it.
1.4No interpretation of this Constitution shall permit extraction, neglect, or conduct materially inconsistent with the Community's regenerative purpose.
Article II
Founding Principles
The five values are not aspirational statements. They are the standard against which every decision, structure, and member is evaluated. The community has no interest in people who cannot live by them.
2.1The core values of Snowflake Sanctuary are Creativity, Growth, Land Stewardship, Beauty, and Responsibility.
2.2Creativity means that members are expected to contribute imagination, problem-solving, and constructive making to the life of the Community.
2.3Growth means personal, ecological, relational, and communal development, with the understanding that stagnation is contrary to the Community's purpose.
2.4Land Stewardship means that the land shall be tended, protected, and improved, and that no member possesses the land in a manner inconsistent with stewardship.
2.5Beauty means that structures, systems, and common spaces shall be built and maintained with care, coherence, and respect for craft.
2.6Responsibility means that members shall honor their commitments to one another, to the land, and to the Community's shared agreements.
Article III
The Land
3.1Snowflake Sanctuary is situated upon approximately forty acres in Snowflake, Arizona, at roughly 5,600 feet above sea level, subject to the exact legal description in the applicable deed and related instruments.
3.2The Community recognizes the physical character of the land: red clay soil, juniper and piñon vegetation, a wash running through the property, existing solar and water infrastructure, and the constraints and opportunities of a high-desert climate.
3.3The wash corridor, water-harvesting features, and all related ecological systems shall be treated as essential commons and protected accordingly.
3.4Permanent improvements shall favor low-impact, regenerative, and locally appropriate methods — clay, stone, juniper, earthbag, adobe, cob. No harmful chemicals. No plastics where avoidable. No electricity dependency without redundancy built in.
3.5The Community shall comply with all applicable Arizona water laws, land-use laws, and permitting requirements before constructing ponds, swales, surface water features, or other regulated improvements.
Article IV
Membership
4.1Membership in Snowflake Sanctuary is a privilege conditioned on adherence to this Constitution, any lawful operating agreements, and the Community's core values.
4.2Membership classes: Founding Members, Permanent Residents, Long-Stay Members, Working Guests, Rotating Volunteers, and Retreat Guests.
4.3Founding Members are those persons whose vision, labor, land contribution, capital contribution, or governance role gave rise to the Community.
4.4Permanent Residents are persons who commit to multi-year residence, substantial labor, and participation in governance and commons maintenance.
4.5Long-Stay Members, Working Guests, and Retreat Guests may be granted defined access, housing, labor, or hospitality rights by agreement, but shall not acquire governance rights except as expressly provided.
4.6No person shall be admitted or retained as a member unless reasonably capable of living in good faith with the Community's values and agreements.
Article V
Governance
The governing principle: decisions should be made as close as possible to the people most affected by them. Personal domain is sovereign. The commons are governed collectively. The distinction between these domains is explicit, not assumed.
5.1The Community shall be governed by the principle that decisions should be made as close as possible to the people most affected by them.
5.2Matters affecting private structures, personal practice, and individual conduct shall ordinarily remain within the domain of the responsible person.
5.3Matters affecting commons property, shared infrastructure, water systems, the goat operation, shared kitchens, land-use corridors, community revenue, and common obligations shall be governed collectively by the voting members.
5.4The Community may establish working circles, stewardship teams, or committees for operations, maintenance, building, hospitality, agriculture, finance, and conflict resolution.
5.5No committee, steward, or founder shall exercise authority beyond the scope expressly granted by this Constitution or a written agreement adopted under it.
Article VI
The Founders
6.1The Founders of Snowflake Sanctuary are Lindsay and John, whose respective roles, capacities, contributions, and rights shall be defined by written agreement and incorporated into the governance structure of the Community.
6.2The Founders acknowledge that their respective contributions include land, labor, capital, planning, stewardship, operations, and community architecture, and that clarity regarding those contributions is essential to the stability of the Community.
6.3Before any permanent structure is erected or any long-term commitment undertaken, the Founders shall memorialize their rights and obligations in a written agreement reviewed for legal sufficiency by counsel licensed in Arizona.
6.4The Founders may retain founding authority with respect to initial formation, but such authority shall be exercised consistently with the long-term interests of the Community and shall be subject to the review and sunset provisions of this Constitution.
6.5The land is presently owned by John and his son, and any land-related agreements shall reflect their ownership rights and the Community's use rights.
6.6This Constitution is designed as a replicable social contract for intentional communities, with modular provisions that may be adapted for other properties and groups while preserving core principles of stewardship, responsibility, and regeneration.
Article VII
Land Use and Stewardship
7.1The land shall be used in a manner that increases fertility, conserves water, reduces fire risk, supports habitat, and improves the long-term productivity and beauty of the property.
7.2Members shall not engage in extractive use of soil, water, biomass, or infrastructure without corresponding replenishment or restorative practice.
7.3Trees, brush, forage, and natural materials may be used only in a manner consistent with sustainable management, ecological regeneration, and the Community's overall plan.
7.4Water-harvesting systems, food forest plantings, and agricultural improvements shall be designed with due regard to climate, rainfall, soil type, and legal restrictions.
7.5The Community may designate conservation zones, production zones, residence zones, hospitality zones, and work zones by written map or plan, which may be revised by Community vote.
Article VIII
Housing and Structures
8.1No permanent structure shall be built without prior approval under the Community's building and land-use process and without compliance with all applicable legal requirements.
8.2The Community shall favor structures that are durable, low-impact, beautiful, climate-appropriate, and capable of serving multiple functions. Materials preference: clay, stone, juniper, earthbag, adobe, cob, metal roofing.
8.3Temporary housing, volunteer housing, guest housing, and resident housing shall be assigned by agreement and may be conditioned on contribution, conduct, duration, and capacity.
8.4Any agreement concerning improvements funded or built by a member shall specify ownership, removal rights, reimbursement rights, and treatment upon departure or dissolution.
8.5No member shall occupy or modify another's structure without permission, nor interfere with commons structures without authorization.
Article IX
Work and Contribution
This is not a passive residence. Membership carries obligations of labor, maintenance, and participation proportionate to role and capacity. The community is built by the people in it.
9.1The Community is not a passive residence; membership carries obligations of labor, maintenance, and participation proportionate to role and capacity.
9.2Each resident or long-stay member shall contribute work in a manner consistent with the needs of the Community: animal care, building, food production, cleaning, repair, hospitality, administration, or education.
9.3The Community may establish labor-credit, revenue-share, stipend, barter, or housing-for-work arrangements by written policy or individual agreement.
9.4Labor expectations for guests or volunteers shall be stated in advance and shall not be exceeded without consent.
9.5No person shall be compelled to perform labor beyond the scope of an agreed arrangement except as reasonably necessary in an emergency affecting life, health, or property.
Article X
Commons and Revenue
10.1The Community may own, operate, or manage commons assets, including kitchens, water systems, animal operations, workshops, gardens, roads, and shared tools.
10.2Any income derived from commons enterprises shall be accounted for transparently and used first for operating expenses, reserves, infrastructure replacement, debt service if any, and agreed distributions.
10.3The goat operation, dairy production, cheesemaking, land-clearing services, hospitality, workshops, and related enterprises may be treated as Community economic activities if so designated by written agreement.
10.4Where labor entitles a person to compensation, profit share, or use rights, the applicable formula shall be stated in writing and administered in good faith.
10.5The Community shall maintain bookkeeping sufficient to show income, expenses, reserves, obligations, and distributions with reasonable clarity.
Additional Articles
Conflict, Review & Replication
The three structural principles derived from the We The People 2026 constitutional framework: regeneration not extraction (Art. 31), decisions closest to the people (Art. 34A), and living generation governance with annual review and sunset provisions (Art. 35).
XI.1Conflict Resolution. Disputes between members shall first be addressed directly. If unresolved, the matter goes to a circle of three community members — one chosen by each party, one chosen by both. The circle's recommendation is advisory but carries significant weight in community decisions.
XI.2Annual Review. Every January, the community gathers to reaffirm, revise, or sunset agreements. Rules that are not actively reaffirmed expire. The living community governs — not the founders' original vision frozen in clay.
XI.3Sunset Provisions. No provision of this Constitution shall govern without periodic reaffirmation. The community exists to serve its living members, not to preserve the preferences of its founders.
XI.4Regeneration Standard. Every practice, structure, and member is evaluated against a single question: does this intercept, convert, and return — or does it simply take? This is the operating principle of every ecosystem that has survived.
XI.5Replicable Model. This Constitution is designed to be adapted for other intentional communities. It is a case study and a template. The teaching mission of Snowflake Sanctuary includes documenting and sharing this governance architecture with communities that wish to build something similar.
