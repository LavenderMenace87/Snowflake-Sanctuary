Resident Homes

Each permanent resident holds their own private space on the land — designed by them, built by them, theirs. The community gathers at the commons. The rest belongs to the individual.

The Model

Sovereign Space Within Shared Land

Snowflake Sanctuary is not a commune. It is a community of sovereign individuals who share infrastructure, land stewardship obligations, and a governing covenant — and who each maintain a private area that is wholly their own. No clustering. No shared walls. Each resident's space is a private node within the larger organism.

The communal kitchen, the wash corridor, the ponds, the food forest, the pottery studio, the art studio — these are commons. Your home, your garden, your creative space — these are yours.

🏔 Private Elevation

Each resident selects their own area of the 40 acres. Sites are chosen to avoid visual intrusion on neighbors, to maximize solar orientation, and to respect the land's hydrology. No two homes share a view corridor.

⬡ Custom-Designed Home

Residents commission their Geoship (or build their own structure) to their own specification. Layout, size, orientation, interior design — all theirs. The only constraint: beauty, durability, and consistency with the land.

🌱 Personal Garden Zone

Each resident has a defined personal garden and growing area adjacent to their home. Complementary to but independent of the communal food forest. You grow what you want, how you want it.

🔐 Ownership Pathway

Permanent residents who build on the land accrue ownership rights in their structure and improvements. The specific threshold and equity framework is defined in the Founders' Agreement and the community covenant.

⚖️ Personal Domain is Sovereign

From Article V of the community constitution: matters affecting private structures, personal practice, and individual conduct remain within the domain of the responsible person. No committee governs your home.

🤝 Commons Contribution

Private sovereignty comes with commons responsibility. Each resident contributes defined hours to shared systems — goat care, food forest, water systems, guest hosting. The formula is transparent and agreed before arrival.

Aspirational Partnership

Geoship — Geodesic Ceramic Homes

Geoship (geoship.is) builds geodesic dome homes from a geopolymer ceramic composite that is stronger than concrete, fire-resistant, non-toxic, and beautiful. The geometry is ancient — Buckminster Fuller's insight translated into permanent residential architecture. The material is modern — engineered to outlast wood-frame construction by centuries.

This is an aspirational partnership — we intend to reach out to Geoship as the community forms and present Snowflake Sanctuary as a showcase site. Every resident who chooses a Geoship home strengthens the case for a volume relationship.

How Someone Becomes a Resident

The Path to Permanent Membership

01 Long-Stay Visit

3–8 weeks. Working guest or artist residency. You contribute labor. You experience the community. The community experiences you.

02 Extended Stay

3–6 months. Deeper integration. You take on a defined role. You participate in governance. The relationship develops in both directions.

03 Membership Proposal

You propose your permanent role, your home site, your contribution model, and your build plan. Founding members review and confirm alignment.

04 Site Selection

You choose your private area. It is mapped, recorded, and protected by the community covenant. No one else builds there.

05 Build + Root

You build your home — Geoship, earth dome, converted structure, or custom build. You plant your garden. You root into the land. After an agreed improvement threshold, you hold equity in your structure.

The Heart of the Commons

The Communal Kitchen

Not a farmhouse kitchen. A production space and gathering place large enough for cheesemaking, fermentation, communal meals, and teaching — built from juniper timber, stone, and clay from the land. The most beautiful structure on the property, and the most used.

Design
  • Juniper timber frame · stone and adobe walls
  • Open-air or semi-open design · pergola extension
  • Wood-fired oven from local clay · center hearth
  • Ironwork fixtures from the forge · hooks, hardware, shelving
  • Herb garden adjacent · growing into the kitchen
  • Large enough for 20+ people to gather comfortably
  • Outdoor seating under juniper canopy or grape vine trellis
Function
  • Cheesemaking: pasteurization sink, mold press, aging area
  • Fermentation alcove: crocks, kimchi, lacto-fermented vegetables
  • Arizona Cottage Food certified for direct sales
  • Daily communal meals for residents + WWOOFers
  • Workshop space: cheesemaking, fermentation, bread
  • Honey extraction · beeswax processing
  • Seasonal preservation: drying, canning, lacto-ferment
Above the Ground

Floating + Elevated Structures

Where the earth dome goes deep, these structures go up. HipCamp premium listings, meditation and art spaces, treehouse lodging along the wash. They set Snowflake Sanctuary apart as a destination — nowhere else looks like this.

🛖 Treehouse · Wash Corridor

Built into the larger junipers along the wash. Cable-anchored for load distribution. Sleeping platform + small sitting area. Off-grid. HipCamp premium listing. The most requested stay.

🛏 Elevated Sleeping Pods (×2)

Off-ground on post foundations. No digging, no foundation. Lightweight timber or salvage frame. Private, insulated, off-grid. Views across the juniper canopy. HipCamp premium.

🧘 Floating Platforms · Crescent Pools

Lightweight cedar or recycled-lumber platforms anchored over the crescent pools. Used for yoga, meditation, painting, small gatherings. Rise and fall with water level on vertical pole anchors.

🌉 Suspended Walkway

Cable-supported bridge between elevated structures. Juniper timber decking, rope rail. Connects treehouse to pod or central point. The most photographed element on the property.

🌿 Chinampa Garden Rafts · Main Pond

2–5 edge-based peninsula beds and semi-floating rafts in the main pond. Reeds + brush + compost layers, root-bound over time. Mint, watercress, chard, kale, cattails. Shade reduces evaporation. Fish waste feeds plants.

🎬 Central Station Deck

John's elevated green screen deck. Built into or adjacent to the central station. Clean sight lines, north-facing diffuse light. Doubles as community presentation space and content creation studio.
